The Algorithm

RIR Periodization.
Adaptive Recovery.
Three Goals.

ARPO uses Reps In Reserve (RIR) periodization — training at progressive RIR across your mesocycle before a structured deload. The algorithm adapts rep targets, weight increments, and intensity thresholds per your goal: Strength (2→1→0 RIR), Powerbuilding, or Hypertrophy (3→2→1 RIR). Combined with per-exercise Adaptive Recovery Ratings (ARR), it knows exactly when to push harder and when to pull back.

What is Auto-Regulated Progressive Overload (ARPO)?

ARPO is a training methodology where your program automatically adjusts volume, intensity, and weight based on how you actually performed — not a fixed schedule. After each set you rate your recovery, and the algorithm recalculates next week's targets. No guesswork, no plateau.

Does it work without an internet connection?

Yes. ARPO is 100% offline-first. Your entire exercise library, workout history, and training data live on your device. No account, no cloud sync, no internet required — ever.

What's the difference between Strength, Powerbuilding, and Hypertrophy?

These are the three training goals you choose when starting a program. Strength trains heavy with 1–5 reps and lower RIR — optimised for peak force output and neural adaptation. Hypertrophy uses 8–15 reps with a higher RIR window to maximise muscle growth through volume and metabolic stress. Powerbuilding sits in between — 5–8 reps on compounds, 8–10 on isolation — building strength and size simultaneously. Any template works with any goal.
