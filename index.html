FAQ

Common questions.

How much does ARPO cost?

ARPO is a one-time purchase — no subscription, no recurring fees, no ads, no paywalled features. Pay once and own it forever. Every new feature ships as a free update.

How is ARPO different from RP Hypertrophy or JeFit?

RP Hypertrophy charges ~$19.99/month ($240/year) and JeFit ~$7.99/month for auto-regulated training. ARPO delivers the same science — plus Apple Health integration and body composition tracking — for a single one-time purchase. It also works 100% offline with no account needed, which neither competitor offers.

Does ARPO sync with Apple Health?

Yes. Tap Sync on the Recovery card and ARPO pulls your resting heart rate, HRV, and sleep hours from Apple Health. A traffic-light status (green / amber / red) tells you your readiness before each session. Steps also sync automatically for streak tracking.

What is Auto-Regulated Progressive Overload?

Your program automatically adjusts volume, intensity, and weight based on how you actually performed — not a fixed schedule. After each set you rate your recovery, and the algorithm recalculates next week's targets. No guesswork, no plateau.

What's the difference between Strength, Powerbuilding, and Hypertrophy?

Strength trains heavy with 1–5 reps and lower RIR — optimised for peak force. Hypertrophy uses 8–15 reps with a higher RIR window to maximise muscle growth. Powerbuilding sits in between — 5–8 reps on compounds, 8–10 on isolation. Any template works with any goal.

Is Android supported?

Not yet — ARPO is currently available on iOS via the App Store. Android support is actively in development. The core app is built in React Native so the port is well underway; expect it in a future release.
