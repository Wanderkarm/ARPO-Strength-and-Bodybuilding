Privacy Policy

ARPO is built around a simple principle: your data belongs to you. This policy explains what information the app accesses, how it is used, and — most importantly — what never leaves your device.

Last updated: April 2026

The Short Version

ARPO does not collect, transmit, or store any personal data on external servers. There are no user accounts, no cloud sync, no analytics SDKs, and no advertising. Every piece of data you enter — workouts, body measurements, health metrics — lives exclusively on your device. Deleting the app deletes everything.

1. Who We Are

ARPO – Strength & Bodybuilding ("ARPO", "we", "our") is an iOS fitness application developed and published by ARPO Strength and Bodybuilding. Questions about this policy can be directed to the contact address at the bottom of this page.

2. Information Stored Locally on Your Device

ARPO stores the following data on-device only using Apple's on-device SQLite database and AsyncStorage. This data never leaves your device unless you explicitly use the in-app backup export feature.

  • Profile information — gender, bodyweight, height, age, training experience, and activity level. Used to estimate starting weights and calculate nutrition targets.
  • Workout data — training plans, exercise logs, set logs, target weights, and RIR ratings. Used to calculate progressive overload week-to-week.
  • Body measurements — weight logs, waist, neck, hip, chest, and arm measurements. Used to calculate body fat percentage and FFMI using the US Navy formula.
  • App preferences — weight unit (kg/lbs), notification settings, step goal, and exercise swap preferences.
  • Health data from Apple Health — see Section 3 below.

3. Apple HealthKit Data

ARPO integrates with Apple Health to display a Recovery readiness score and daily step count. The following data types may be accessed with your explicit permission:

  • Read: Resting Heart Rate — used to assess cardiovascular recovery status.
  • Read: Heart Rate Variability (HRV) — used to assess nervous system recovery status.
  • Read: Sleep Analysis — sleep duration used to assess rest quality.
  • Read: Step Count — daily step count displayed against your personal step goal.
  • Write: Workout sessions may optionally be logged to Apple Health.

HealthKit data is never used for advertising, marketing, or any purpose other than displaying your recovery readiness within the app. HealthKit data is never shared with third parties, never transmitted off your device, and never combined with data from other apps. This is a requirement of Apple's HealthKit guidelines and a core principle of how ARPO is built.

You can revoke HealthKit permissions at any time in your iPhone's Settings → Privacy & Security → Health → ARPO.

4. Data You Export

ARPO includes a Backup & Restore feature that lets you export your data as a JSON file. This file is generated on-device and shared using your device's native share sheet — you choose where it goes (Files, iCloud, email, etc.). We never receive, store, or have access to your backup files.

5. Notifications

ARPO may request permission to send local push notifications — for example, workout reminders or weigh-in prompts. These notifications are scheduled entirely on-device. No notification data is transmitted to external servers. You can disable notifications at any time in Settings → Notifications → ARPO.

6. Third-Party Services

ARPO does not integrate with any third-party analytics, advertising, or crash-reporting services. The app contains no tracking SDKs.

The app is distributed through the Apple App Store. Apple may collect certain usage and diagnostic data as described in Apple's Privacy Policy. This is separate from ARPO and outside our control.

7. Children's Privacy

ARPO is not directed at children under 13 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided personal information through the app, please contact us and we will take appropriate steps.

8. Data Retention & Deletion

All data is stored locally on your device. You can delete all data at any time via Settings → Danger Zone → Delete All Data within the app. Deleting the app from your device also removes all associated data. We have no copy of your data and cannot recover it after deletion.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. Material changes will be communicated via an in-app notice or App Store update notes. Continued use of ARPO after changes constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
